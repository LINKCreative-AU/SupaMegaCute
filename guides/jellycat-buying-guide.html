The Jellycat Buying Guide: Most Popular Plush & How to Choose

Hundreds of characters, a lot of sold-out favourites, and one very soft decision. Here's how to choose your Jellycat without spiralling.

If you have ever pressed your face into a Jellycat and made an involuntary noise, welcome. You are among friends. Jellycat is the London-born plush house that turned "impossibly soft" into a personality, and their range has grown from a few sleepy bunnies into a menagerie of smiling eggplants, giggling avocados, and startlingly huggable crabs. The catch is that there are hundreds of them, they sell out, and half the fun is deciding where to start.

Start with the classics: Bashful and Amuseable

Two families do most of the heavy lifting in the Jellycat world. The Bashful line is the animal side of the house: droopy-eared bunnies, sleepy foxes, dozy kittens, all with that signature soft-fur finish and slightly bashful tilt. If someone pictures "a Jellycat" in their head, it is almost certainly a Bashful Bunny. The Amuseable line is the whimsical side: everyday objects and foods given little faces and stubby legs. Bashful is the comforting choice; Amuseable is the make-someone-laugh choice.

Size matters more than you think

Jellycat sizing is one of the most common sources of buyer surprise, because "Medium" and "Really Big" are doing a lot of work. A few rules of thumb:

  • Small / Little — bag-friendly, desk-friendly, great as an add-on or a first plush for a small human.
  • Medium — the sweet spot for most people. Substantial enough to hug, not so big it needs its own chair.
  • Large and up — statement territory. Wonderful, but measure your shelf (and your heart) first.

When in doubt, size up one notch from what the photo suggests. Product images have a way of making everything look medium.

How to choose the right one

The trick to choosing is to stop browsing the entire catalog and start with the person. Are you buying for a newborn, where softness and simplicity win? A teen who wants the trendy one everyone is posting? A friend who collects a specific animal? Match the plush to the human and the field narrows fast. Caring for your Jellycat

Jellycats are cuddled hard, which means they eventually need a wash. The general guidance is gentle: surface-clean with a damp cloth for everyday grime, and avoid throwing them in a hot machine, which can mat that lovely fur. Let them air dry fully rather than tumble drying, keep them out of prolonged direct sunlight, and give ears and limbs a gentle reshape after any wash. Treated kindly, a Jellycat holds its softness for years.

When to buy (and when to grab it)

Jellycat rotates its range regularly, retiring designs and introducing seasonal characters, which is why that avocado you liked last spring may have quietly vanished. There is no shame in the rule of cute shopping: if it makes you smile and it is in stock, that is often the whole decision. For more squishy companions beyond the Jellycat shelf, the whole collectibles world is waiting.
