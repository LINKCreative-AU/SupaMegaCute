Squishmallows vs Jellycat vs Pusheen: The Plush Showdown

Three plush titans, three completely different philosophies of cute. Here's how they actually differ, so you pick the right one.

Three plush titans, three completely different philosophies of cute. Squishmallows want to be squished. Jellycats want to be cuddled. Pusheen wants to sit on your desk being relatable and slightly lazy. They all make excellent companions, but they are genuinely not interchangeable, and buying the wrong one for the wrong person leads to that polite "oh, thank you" face nobody enjoys.

Texture: the whole personality

Texture is where these three separate fastest. Squishmallows are marshmallow-like: a dense, springy foam-style fill wrapped in a soft velvety shell. You squeeze them and they push back. Jellycat goes the opposite direction, with a plush, luxuriously fuzzy fur and a softer, more relaxed fill designed for hugging and draping rather than squishing. Pusheen sits in between: a classic soft toy feel, huggable and pleasant, but the draw is really the character. If your giftee is a tactile person who fidgets, Squishmallow. If they want something to fall asleep holding, Jellycat.

Character vs. collection

These brands also answer "why do I want this" differently. Pusheen is a character first: a chubby grey tabby with a specific personality. Squishmallows are a collection: each one is a named individual with a little bio, and the fun is in the hunt. Jellycat lands between the two, with the emphasis firmly on the object itself being beautiful and soft.

  • Buying for a collector who loves the hunt? Squishmallows, every time.
  • Buying for someone who quotes the character and loves the lore? Pusheen.
  • Buying a keepsake-quality cuddle companion? Jellycat.

Size and space

All three come in a range of sizes, but they scale differently. Squishmallows are famous for their generous large sizes — the big ones become genuine furniture. Jellycat spans tiny to enormous, with the medium sizes being the everyday sweet spot. Pusheen tends to live in the small-to-medium desk-and-bed range. Before you buy the biggest option available, picture where it will actually live.

Which one is the better gift?

There is no universal winner, only the right match. For a young child or a fidget-friendly gift, Squishmallows are hard to beat. For a milestone gift or someone who wants a companion to keep for years, Jellycat brings that keepsake feel. For a cat person or a desk-decorator, Pusheen is the obvious delight. The verdict

Think of it this way: Squishmallows are for squeezing, Jellycats are for hugging, and Pusheen is for loving a specific chubby cat. None is wrong, and honestly, the most common outcome of this comparison is deciding you need one of each.
